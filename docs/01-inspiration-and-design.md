## Core Experience Principles

The site should **feel like an experience**, not just a page to read.

### The Three Pillars

| Pillar | Inspiration | How It Manifests |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Hover Reveals** | Dennis Snellenberg | Project cards that come alive, smooth image reveals, satisfying interactions |
| **Immersive 3D** | Lusion | Hero scene and key moments use purposeful 3D, not decoration |
| **Scroll Storytelling** | All three | Content unfolds as a journey, each scroll reveals something new |

### Experience Goals

1. **First 3 seconds**: Visitor is intrigued by 3D hero, wants to explore
2. **First scroll**: Realizes this isn't a typical portfolio, it's an experience
3. **Hover on project**: Delighted by the reveal animation
4. **Throughout**: Smooth, intentional, every interaction feels crafted
5. **After leaving**: Remembers the site, wants to come back

### What "Experience" Means Here

- NOT a game to play
- NOT flashy for flash's sake
- NOT overwhelming or busy
- YES an intentional journey
- YES moments of delight
- YES memorable and distinctive
- YES smooth and refined

## Hero Section Concepts

### Option A: "Altitude View"
- 3D scene of clouds/landscape from cockpit perspective
- As you scroll, you "descend" through clouds into content
- Parallax layers create depth
- Your name appears as if on a flight instrument

### Option B: "The Mixer"
- Subtle 3D DJ mixer/soundboard aesthetic
- Sections are "channels" that you can explore
- Audio visualizer reacts to ambient sound or plays subtle beats
- Slider-like navigation elements

### Option C: "The Workshop"
- 3D desk/workspace with objects representing your interests
- Interactive objects: click headphones → music section, click camera → travel, etc.
- Items float and rotate subtly
- More minimal version of bruno-simon approach

### Option D: "The Map"
- Abstract topographic/flight path visualization
- Shows your journey (literally and metaphorically)
- Points of interest reveal content sections
- GPS/waypoint aesthetic
